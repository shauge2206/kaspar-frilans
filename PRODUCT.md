# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Editorial decision-makers in Norway, in three overlapping pools: trade-press editors (notably fisheries / maritime publications like Fiskeribladet, Intrafish, Kystens Næringsliv), business-press editors, and investigative / news-desk editors. They land on the site briefly — usually after a referral, a byline, or a Google for the name — and decide within seconds whether Kaspar Knudsen is the kind of journalist they trust with an assignment. Most are time-poor, skim-first, and read on desktop at work; some forward the URL to a colleague before responding.

## Product Purpose

A freelance journalist's personal portfolio. The site exists so editors can quickly see who Kaspar is, what he has written, where to find him, and reach out — by email or phone — without friction. Success on a first visit is one of three actions: the editor emails or calls him; the editor remembers the name and finds it again later; the editor forwards the URL to a colleague who might commission him.

Not in scope: blog publishing, content marketing, lead capture funnels, newsletter sign-ups, social-media engagement, audience building. This is a credentialing surface, not a media product.

## Brand Personality

Serious, timeless, professional.

Serious: the design must read as competent and considered, not playful or decorative. The voice is direct, the typography is settled, the layout doesn't try to entertain. A journalist's craft is taken seriously; the site signals the same.

Timeless: visual decisions that would have looked confident in 1995 and will still look confident in 2030. Editorial conventions over design-trend conventions. No effects that will date the site within a year.

Professional: clean execution at every touchpoint. Real content, real names, real publications. No demo copy, no placeholder feel, no visible compromises. The site itself is a writing sample.

The emotional register is closer to a museum wall label than a marketing landing page — confident, quiet, and self-evident.

## Anti-references

- **Squarespace / Wix portfolio templates.** Centred hero photo, big-script tagline, three feature cards, scroll-snap testimonials. The generic creative-portfolio template that signals "I bought a theme."
- **Glossy influencer / personal-brand pages.** Hero portrait at 80% viewport height, name in display-cursive, links to socials, "work with me" CTA, soft pastel gradients. Reads as self-promotional rather than substantive.
- **Generic SaaS marketing landings.** Gradient hero, three benefit columns with icons, "trusted by" logo strip, animated product mockup, dark mode toggle in the corner. Reads as a product company, not a person.

If a visiting editor could mistake this site for any of those three categories, the design has failed.

## Design Principles

1. **Show the work, not the personality.** Editors decide on bylines and beats, not vibes. Clips, publications, story types, and contact details earn more space than self-description.
2. **Restraint is the credential.** Every decorative choice spends seriousness. The design proves competence by not trying — strong typography, generous whitespace, very few flourishes, and only those that are deliberate.
3. **The name is the anchor.** Typography and structure should make "Kaspar Knudsen" the thing visitors leave with. Layout, hierarchy, and repetition all reinforce identity over feature breadth.
4. **Editorial conventions beat design-trend conventions.** When choosing between an idiom from print journalism (mastheads, kicker labels, ruled margins, justified body, captions) and one from contemporary web design (gradients, glassmorphism, scroll storytelling), pick the editorial one.
5. **Friction-free contact is the conversion.** Email and phone should be one tap or one glance away on every page. No contact form gating, no "schedule a call" widgets, no newsletter opt-in interrupting the flow.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target **WCAG 2.2 AA**. Practical implications for this site:

- Contrast ratio ≥ 4.5:1 for normal-size body text and ≥ 3:1 for large text and UI components, on every theme.
- Visible focus rings on all interactive elements, with non-color cues.
- Respect `prefers-reduced-motion` — page transitions and word-reveal animations must degrade to a static state.
- Full keyboard navigability with logical tab order; no keyboard traps.
- Sufficient text-resize headroom (200% zoom without horizontal scroll on standard breakpoints).
- Norwegian-language content marked `lang="nb"` for screen readers.
- Color is never the sole channel for meaning; theme accents must work for users with color-vision differences.
